OpenRouter benchmark - Clear / Meph

Gemini is the cheap default. Opus is the quality ceiling.

Six models were tested on five Meph-shaped tasks: Clear app generation, compiler-error repair, requirements extraction, Clear gotcha detection, and app generation with canonical Clear syntax examples.

Task Design

The tasks were chosen to match the work Meph actually does, not generic trivia.

1. Clear app, no syntax context Tests whether the model can infer Clear from English alone. Most models failed by inventing a different language.
2. Compiler-error repair Tests exactness. The correct fix required the full allowlisted endpoint, including scheme and path.
3. Requirements extraction Tests product judgment: roles, data, acceptance checks, auditability, and task slicing.
4. Clear gotchas Tests whether the model catches route-order bugs, weak update targeting, and no-op guards.
5. Clear app with syntax context Most important for Meph. The model gets canonical examples first, then writes a small app.

Bottom Line

Gemini is not secretly as good as Opus. It missed the exact compiler-error fix and its generated Clear app did not compile. But it delivered about 72 percent of Opus's judged quality at 7 percent of the cost and about three times the speed.

For Meph, that means Gemini should handle cheap everyday drafting. Opus should be the escalation model for "make it actually compile", gnarly bug repair, and high-stakes app generation.
